Programs


We work, study, collaborate, and explore it all in our national parks. We protect our most special places through innovative and collaborative projects.

The National Park Federation (NPF) plays a vital role in supporting and enhancing the United States' national parks through various programs aimed at conservation, education, and community engagement. By advocating for policies that protect natural and cultural resources, the NPF works to secure funding and resources necessary for park maintenance and development.

Its initiatives, such as "Parks for All" and youth engagement programs, foster inclusivity and promote environmental stewardship among diverse communities. Additionally, NPF partners with local organizations to implement conservation projects that restore habitats, ensuring that these treasured landscapes are preserved for future generations to explore and enjoy.

The National Park Foundation's programs are designed to connect people with the beauty and significance of America’s national parks while fostering a sense of responsibility toward their preservation. Initiatives like "Parks Connect" facilitate partnerships with local communities, promoting collaborative efforts to protect park resources and enhance visitor engagement. The foundation also champions the "National Park Service's Urban Agenda," which focuses on bringing the benefits of parks to urban populations, ensuring that city dwellers have access to natural spaces.

Through funding and support for projects that improve infrastructure, visitor services, and educational opportunities, the National Park Foundation plays a crucial role in enhancing the sustainability and accessibility of these cherished landscapes for all.

Our Programs

Accessibility for All Americans

Ensuring our national parks are open and inclusive to everyone safeguards a vibrant, thriving future for the parks. Funds secured today can ensure all Americans with disabilities participate in the life and ecosystem of our national parks.

Conservating Water

It is our responsibility to preserve fresh water — a vital limited resource that preserves park ecosystems — while reaping the simultaneous benefits of reduction in energy use from water treatment and transport.

Habitat Conservation

Many national parks were created because of their unique landscapes and their biodiverse ecosystems that host a rich variety of wildlife and plants – some of which can’t be found anywhere else in the world. National parks are home to an abundant complexity of terrestrial and aquatic habitats, often acting as a refuge for wildlife. But even protected lands are not immune to threats.

Internships

Internships play a critical role in developing the talent pool of diverse, enthusiastic, motivated, and highly skilled young people who are next in line to take care of the more than 400 national park sites that make up the national park system.

Reducing Waste, Cleaning Parks

The National Park Foundation supports solutions to fund waste diversion projects like recycling and composting to help support our goal of diverting 75% of waste from landfills anually.
